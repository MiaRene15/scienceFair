Being Present for Anxiousness

Length: 5:24 (minutes:seconds)

Reasoning: Anxiousness is defined as focusing on the future or frightening possibilities. The act of being present reduces anxiousness and increases positivity. I believe a guided exercise focused on being present will bring individuals feeling anxiousness to a more positive mindset.

Background Research: A Harvard University study showed that adults spend only 50% of their time focusing on the present. It also found that when individuals are fully present, they are at their happiest. Other research reveals that being present makes you more productive, charismatic, and happier.

Directions: You may stand or sit in the chair for this simulation. If you don't already have VR Goggles on, please click start and put them on right away.
