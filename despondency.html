Gratitude for Despondency

Length: 5:43 (minutes:seconds)

Reasoning: Research shows gratitude gives an abundance of happiness. Despondency is almost like the lack of happiness. I believe a guided meditation about gratitude will bring individuals experiencing despondency to a more positive mindset.

Background Research: Research proves that people who are consistently grateful are more joyful and healthy. Greater Good Science Center at University of California, Berkeley conducted a study where people expressed thanks every day for about two weeks. They learned that "days with more gratitude featured more positive emotions...and fewer...negative emotions." Groups that were consistent with being grateful experienced less congestion, stomach pain, cough, sore throats and headaches than others. Gratefulness makes you mentally and physically better.
